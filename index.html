Coaching Methodology
CAL DIETZ · TRIPHASIC
Primary structure. Eccentric phase (Wks 1–2) → Isometric/pause phase (Wks 3–4) → Concentric/speed phase (Wks 5–8). Each phase drives both hypertrophy AND athletic development simultaneously. No trade-off.
DAN BAKER · RUGBY SCIENCE
17 years of published rugby research. Bench press and deadlift 1RM are the top discriminators of playing standard. Power output against high external loads separates levels. Programmed as primary lifts.
SIMMONS / SOVIET · CONJUGATE
Speed-strength is never absent. Even in hypertrophy phases a small power primer keeps the CNS athletic. In-season (Wks 9–16): concurrent interference minimised by 24h+ separation between gym and pitch.
BOYLE + ATG · UNILATERAL
Sport happens in split stance. Left leg leads every unilateral. ATG: full ROM, knee-over-toe. Research: targeted unilateral training significantly reduces limb asymmetry in 10 weeks.
Weeks 9–16 Concurrent Training
Why the interference effect won't kill your gains: Baker's 19-week study on professional rugby players found no reduction in strength or power during in-season concurrent training when sessions were properly sequenced. Your gym is Monday/Friday mornings, pitch is Tuesday/Thursday evenings — that's 24+ hours separation, which research confirms effectively eliminates the interference effect.
In-season gym structure (Wks 9–16): Two full-body sessions per week (Mon + Fri). Higher intensity, lower volume than pre-season. Compounds stay at 80–85% intensity. Power work maintained. The pitch sessions replace conditioning volume — the gym now purely maintains and builds strength/mass. Studies show well-trained athletes can continue gaining on 2 sessions/week if intensity stays high.
